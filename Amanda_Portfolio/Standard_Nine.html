Standard Nine: The teacher is a reflective practitioner who continually evaluates the effect of his or her choices and actions on pupils, parents, professionals in the learning community and others and who actively seeks out opportunities to grow professionally.



Artifacts:  Reflective Teaching Journal Entries

                         Developed while teaching at Prairie View Elementary School (2007/2008)

                     WI DPI Professional Development Plan

                         Developed while teaching at Prairie View Elementary School (2007/2008)

               

Ability to Think Reflectively to Improve Teaching: My reflective teaching journal entries address my feelings about the success of lessons I taught while teaching at Prairie View Elementary in 2007/2008.  In the reflections I discuss how my lessons impacted student learning and how they helped the students progress toward the student learning objectives.  In addition, I discuss what went well during the lessons and should be continued in the future, as well as things that I felt could be improved upon.  I explain why I felt concern and possible steps I could take to improve my teaching and create a better experience for my students in future lessons.  My writing represents my ability to think reflectively and use my experiences to help me learn, change, and grow as a teacher in order to create the best learning experiences for my students!

   

Ability to Think Reflectively to Foster Professional Growth:  The professional development plan that I developed while teaching at Prairie View Elementary demonstrates my ability to think reflectively about my teaching abilities and brainstorm areas where I may need to grow professionally to help create better learning experiences for my students.  In my PDP, I use reflection to create goals that address areas I feel may still need improvement and growth.  I then created a timeline that lists various objectives for reaching my goal and the steps I will take to help myself grow professionally as a teacher.  By doing so, I hope to never stop learning and growing as a professional.
