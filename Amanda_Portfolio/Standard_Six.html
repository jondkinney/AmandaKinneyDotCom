Standard Six:

The teacher uses effective verbal and nonverbal communication techniques as well as instructional media and technology to foster active inquiry, collaboration, and supportive interaction in the classroom.



Artifacts:  Lesson Plan, Men’s Choir, November 28, 2006

                         Developed while student teaching at Chippewa Falls High School

                     Mid-Quarter and End-Of-Quarter Assessment Profile

                         Completed by Mrs. Karin Boumeester while student teaching at Sherman Elementary

               

Knowledge of Effective Interactions:  I believe it is very important as a teacher to know your students on a personal level.  Short conversations or questioning about a student’s life outside of school shows that you care for your students as individuals.  Before and after each class period, I try to talk to my students and learn about their interests and needs in my class.  In the lesson plan I designed for Men’s Choir at Chippewa Falls High School, I used my knowledge of individual students’ interest in pop and jazz music to foster active inquiry about the origins and vocal styles of the traditional spiritual.  Through discussion, the students talked about the origins of the spiritual and came up with characteristics that describe most spirituals.  We also listened to a recording of Aretha Franklin (brought in by one of the students) to get a better idea of the vocal timbre of the African American race.  Together the students and I were able to explore the history and performance practice of one of their pieces through collaboration and active participation.


Knowledge of Effective Communication: My mid-quarter and end-of-quarter evaluations, completed by my cooperating teacher, Ms. Karin Boumeester, demonstrate my ability to use my knowledge of effective verbal and nonverbal communication to create meaningful and understandable lesson for my students.  In the categories of instruction and professionalism, I was rated at the highest competency level for the following subcategories:

•Articulates clear learning goals to students.

•Structures content and conveys it in an articulate manner.

•Maintains effective verbal and nonverbal communication.

•Communicates assessment criteria and processes.

•Displays self-confidence and assertiveness.

•Maintains bearing and demeanor appropriate for the environment.

•Conveys a professional attitude.

I feel that it is important that teachers speak and write in a grammatically correct and expressive manner.  They should understand the vocabulary level of their students and use language that the students will understand and is meaningful to them.  During my teaching, I adapt my vocabulary and speaking methods to the developmental level of each of my classes, and by doing so, I feel that I am a clear, articulate teacher.
