Standard Three: 

The teacher understands how pupils differ in their approaches to learning and the barriers that impede learning and can adapt instruction to meet the diverse needs of pupils, including those with disabilities and exceptionalities.



Artifacts:  Lesson Plan, Grade 4, March 2 - 4, 2009

                         Developed while teaching at Prairie View Elementary School

               

Knowledge of Students’ Varied Approaches to Learning: The lesson plan that I developed for my fourth grade students at Prairie View Elementary demonstrates my ability to plan and use varied approaches in instructional planning to meet the needs of all students.  The plan uses a variety of activities that specifically target visual, aural, and kinesthetic learners and many of the activities use strategies that stimulate all three types of learners.  Also, the activities of the lesson are planned in quick succession to help students, especially those with behavioral disabilities, remain attentive, engaged, and on-task.  The students alternate from sitting and listening activities to movement and interactive games and practice.  Students are constantly on the move using different modes of learning to experience and understand a concept! 


In addition, each year I gather information from the special education teachers in regards to students on their caseloads and their specific disabilities.  I brainstorm with them accommodations that I can make for students in my classroom, as well as discuss their areas of strength.  Through a cooperative effort, I try to make the appropriate adaptations so all students, even those with disabilities and exceptionalities can excel in the music classroom.
