Standard Eight:

The teacher understands and uses formal and informal assessment strategies to evaluate and ensure the continuous intellectual, social, and physical development of the pupil.



Artifacts:  Lesson Plan, Grade 2, February 19 - 26, 2008

                         Developed while teaching at Prairie View Elementary School

                     Written Assessments, Grades 1 - 4

                         Developed while teaching at Prairie View Elementary School

               

Congruence with Instructional Goals:  The lesson plan that I developed for my second grade students while teaching at Prairie View Elementary demonstrates my ability to use informal assessment methods in congruence with the instructional goals of the lesson.  In this lesson the students were working on the student learning objective: students can define and identify melody and harmony.  To help the students achieve this instructional objective, the students created their own harmony by singing in a round, reviewed the definitions for melody and harmony, and reviewed the visual pictures they had been associating with the two terms.  They were then challenged to listen to various musical examples and identify melody or harmony with yarn strips.  These activities gave me an ideal means to assess the students’ comprehension of the instructional objective of the lesson.  Using informal assessment methods and direct questioning, I was able to assess each student’s performance and thus their comprehension of the instructional goal.


The written assessments I developed for my students at Prairie View Elementary demonstrate my ability to use formal assessment methods.  These worksheets were an excellent way for me to formally check each student’s progress on their student learning objectives.
