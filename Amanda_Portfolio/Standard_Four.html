Standard Four:

The teacher understands and uses a variety of instructional strategies, including the use of technology to encourage children’s developmental of critical thinking, problem solving, and performance skills.



Artifacts:  Lesson Plan, Concert Choir, November 27, 2006

                         Developed while student teaching at Chippewa Falls High School

               

Learning Activities: The lesson plan that I developed for the Concert Choir at Chippewa Falls High School demonstrates my ability to use a variety of instructional strategies to encourage the students’ development of critical thinking and performance skills.  In this lesson, we use a staccato/legato singing strategy to help the students think critically and determine which line(s) in the music is the most important and should be brought out from the overall texture.  I also teach the students the 2/4 conducting pattern to help them feel the beat, as well as physically demonstrate the light, detached style of singing they should be performing.  In the lesson, we also use discussion to analyze the mood in various sections of the piece and how the composers’ compositional techniques create this mood.  Lastly, we use different physical arrangements of the sections within the choir to help the students hear the alternation of the melody from voice to voice.  Through the different activities, the students clearly progress toward the knowledge, skill, and affective outcomes of the unit.
