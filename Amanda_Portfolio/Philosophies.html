Why Have Music In Our Schools?

Music is an important part of every child’s education.

  1. •  Music develops students’ aesthetic experience.

  2. •Music creates an opportunity for students to   explore their emotions as well as their own humanness and inner self.

  3. •Music is a universal phenomenon that has been present in all cultures throughout the known historical development of the human species.

  4. •Music is a unique multiple intelligence that allows many individuals to interact with their inner and outer worlds and construct knowledge.

  5. •Music helps students develop the critical skills of teamwork, discipline, perseverance, dependability, composure, courage, and pride in results.

Music is an integral part of every person’s life and should therefore be considered an essential part of the curriculum.


My Teaching Philosophy

  1. • I believe that all humans have worth and that each and every child can grow and learn.

  2. • I support the use of multiple intelligences, experiential learning, and constructivist practice in the classroom.

  3. • I am a strong advocate of the Kodály method for teaching music, which is an experience-based approach to teaching and learning that focuses on the developmental readiness of the students and a well-sequenced curriculum.

  4. • I believe that students learn best by ‘doing’ and that learning through experience and discovery provides the most meaningful and relevant knowledge for students.

  5. •Learning should be fun!  Much of my teaching focuses around activities and games that are not only fun, but create experiences that spark questions and interest among students.
