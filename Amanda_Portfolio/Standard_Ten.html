Standard Ten:

The teacher fosters relationships with school colleagues, parents, and agencies in the larger community to support pupil learning and well being and who acts with integrity, fairness, and in an ethical manner.


Artifacts:  Various Concert and Musical Program and Pictures

                     Chippewa Valley Youth Choir Program

                         Developed while working with the Chippewa Valley Youth Choirs (2004-2006)

               

Relationships with Colleagues: The programs and pictures in the above attachments demonstrate my interest and ability to form relationships with my colleagues.  My involvement with the teachers, administrators, and custodians at Prairie View was widespread and crucial in planning and performing both the 2007 and 2008 all-school music programs.  Extensive and thorough preparation as well as communicating my thoughts and visions to all teachers and staff involved allowed us to work together to achieve a common goal!  In addition, I work extensively with the fourth and second grade teachers at Prairie View Elementary School to create student productions.  Through our collaboration and teamwork we perform four second grade musicals and a joint fourth grade Pioneer Day closing ceremony each year.  Throughout the school year, I am also able to collaborate with the music and chorus teachers throughout the district.  We plan and organize joint chorus and orchestra concerts, as well as a district wide Spring Sing.  I believe that the best teaching involves teamwork and a sense of collaboration and support among staff members!


Knowledge of Teacher Roles Outside of School: The Chippewa Valley Youth Choir concert programs demonstrate my involvement in activities for students outside the classroom.  I believe it is important for teachers to be involved beyond the classroom activities and to encourage their students to do the same.  Many extracurricular activities can engage and enhance student learning in ways that the traditional classroom may not be able to provide.
