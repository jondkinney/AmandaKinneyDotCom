Standard Seven:

The teacher organizes and plans systematic instruction based upon knowledge of subject matter, pupils, the community, and curriculum goals.



Artifacts:  Lesson Plan, Grade 3, January 3 - 21, 2009

                         Developed while teaching at Prairie View Elementary School

                     First Quarter Unit Plan, Grade 4

                         Developed while student teaching at Sherman Elementary

               

Lesson Structure:  The lesson plan that I developed for my third grade students at Prairie View Elementary demonstrates my ability to create lessons that have clearly defined structure and reasonable time allocations.  When planning the lesson, I took into account the curriculum goals, the instructional objectives of the lesson, and the level and diversity of the students.  I then created a lesson that addressed these goals and objectives while also providing students a variety of activities that stimulate the various learners in the classroom.  The activities were placed in a specific order to allow for a progression of knowledge and skills throughout the lesson.  In addition the activities had small links that eased the students’ transition from activity to activity, as well as showed how previously discussed concepts were used in new material.  I then carefully estimated how much time each part of the lesson would take to assure that I had enough teaching material.  In addition to planning the lesson in general, I also planned adjustments that could be made to the lesson if the activities did not meet the needs of all students. 


Unit Structure:  The unit plan that I designed for my fourth grade students while student teaching at Sherman Elementary demonstrates my ability to plan a unit that contains a logical progression of lessons that reflect the goals of the Eau Claire School District Music Curriculum and the Kodály Method yearly objectives.  The unit that I constructed is based on the constructivist model and backward design process.  Throughout the unit, the students participate in a variety of activities that progressively help them answer the essential questions of the unit as well as progress toward the student learning targets.  Each week the students build upon their knowledge from previous lessons to develop new understandings and skills.  The progression of lessons also provides the students with the resources to successfully complete the assessments scheduled throughout the unit.
