Standard Two: 

The teacher understands how children with broad ranges of ability learn and provides instruction that supports their intellectual, social, and personal development.



Artifacts:  CMP Unit Plan, “Out of the Orient Crystal Skies” Richard Zgodava

                         Developed while student teaching at Chippewa Falls High School

                Lesson Plan, Grade One, November 8 - 13, 2008

                        Developed while student teaching at Prairie View Elementary School


Knowledge of Characteristics of the Age Group: The CMP unit plan that I created for Concert Choir while student teaching at Chippewa Falls High School demonstrates my ability to plan and deliver units of instruction that meet the student’s developmental needs.  When choosing knowledge, skill, and affective outcomes for this unit, I carefully considered the students’ prior knowledge and skill sets, as well as their physical and cognitive developmental levels.  I then created outcomes that would build upon the students’ previous knowledge, challenge the students to reach new understandings, and help them develop new skills.  All of the outcomes chosen should be readily achievable for students at the 9-12 grade levels.


The lesson plan I created for my first grade students at Prairie View Elementary demonstrates my ability to plan and deliver lessons that meet elementary students’ developmental needs.  In this lesson, I used my understanding of the students’ current skill and developmental levels to help them explore and then construct an understanding of the rhythms ‘ta’ and ‘ti-ti’.  Throughout the lesson, I carefully considered the students’ previous knowledge of terminology, their skills levels, and attention span in planning a lesson that was thought provoking, creative and engaging for young students.
