Personal Philosophy Statement on the
Importance of Music Education

"Music education opens doors that help children pass from school into the world around them - a world of work, culture, intellectual activity, and human involvement. The future of our nation depends on providing our children with a complete education that includes music."

Gerald Ford, former President of the United States

Music education is a necessity in all students’ lives and should be included in schools’ curricula.  Music is a unique art form that provides students with essential experiences that enhance their lives in various ways.

A critical purpose of a music education program is to develop every student’s aesthetic experience to its highest potential.  Music and the arts have an intrinsic value that creates an opportunity for students to have meaningful and heartfelt experiences.  Music helps students explore, express, and deal with feelings and emotions that are related to subjective materials at a level and a depth that no other subject is able to achieve.  In addition, music provides students with a creative outlet for their own expression and allows students to safely release their emotions in a very powerful way.   This aesthetic experience becomes a means to self-understanding that allows the student to explore his or her own humanness and inner self.

The sounds and materials of music are representations of the uniquely human aesthetic core.  Susanne Langer states that the tonal structures of music are very similar to the forms of human feeling, for "music is a tonal analogue of emotive life".  Therefore by including music in schools’ curriculums students are given the opportunity to explore and develop the human spirit within them.  They are able to safely investigate and express their feelings and emotions, as well as connect with other students who may be feeling the same way.  By giving the students the opportunity to develop their inner selves through this distinctive art form, students come to better understand what makes them uniquely human - emotion.

In addition to the aesthetic experience, music is a necessary part of every school’s curriculum because it is a constant.  Music is a universal phenomenon that has been present in all cultures, at all times, and throughout the known historical development of the human species.  Music has been found in every known culture that has been discovered and exists in every modern culture today.  Therefore, it is only logical to study something that is so universal and timeless.

Also, according to Howard Gardner’s Theory of Multiple Intelligences, music has unique properties as a form of human intelligence.  For some individuals, musical intelligence is a primary way of knowing and understanding the world around them and in all individuals, musical intelligence and potential can be developed.  Music provides a knowledge system that allows many individuals to interact with their inner and outer worlds and construct knowledge.  Consequently, schools have an obligation to provide instruction for those who find musical interaction to be a primary mode of intelligence.  Failure to do so leaves many students lacking an important means of interacting with, understanding, and improving themselves and the world around them and therefore diminishes the potential for those members.

Finally, participation in music helps students develop the critical skills of teamwork, discipline, perseverance, dependability, composure, courage, and pride in results.  All of these abilities are crucial components of contributing and successful citizens in our world.

Music is an integral part of every person’s life and should therefore be considered an essential part of the curriculum.  Music is a primary means for students to develop their aesthetic experience and explore their emotions.  Music is also a universal phenomenon that links all cultures of the world together.  Lastly, music is a type of intelligence that can and should be developed in all students, and it helps develop important skills and abilities that all students need.  Every student deserves a complete education and that includes one with music as part of the core curriculum.


~Amanda Kinney
